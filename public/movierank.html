1. Oppenheimer


Oppenheimer- movie summary.

Oppenheimer (2023), directed by Christopher Nolan, is a biographical drama centered on J. Robert Oppenheimer, the theoretical physicist who led the Manhattan Project and developed the first atomic bomb during World War II. The film explores Oppenheimer's complex personality, his moral dilemmas, and the far-reaching consequences of his work, especially regarding nuclear warfare. As the father of the atomic bomb, he grapples with the ethical and personal toll of unleashing such devastating power, while navigating political scrutiny and internal conflicts within the scientific community. The movie delves into themes of responsibility, ambition, and the impact of science on humanity.

2. Killers of the Flower Moon

Killers of the Flower Moon- movie summary

Killers of the Flower Moon (2023), directed by Martin Scorsese, is a historical crime drama based on the true events surrounding the Osage Nation murders in the 1920s. The film, adapted from David Grann’s non-fiction book, chronicles the brutal killings of Osage Native Americans in Oklahoma after oil is discovered on their land, making them some of the wealthiest people in America at the time.
The story focuses on the relationship between Mollie Burkhart, an Osage woman, and her white husband, Ernest Burkhart, as a conspiracy involving local landowners unfolds. The murders, orchestrated for financial gain, attract the attention of the newly formed FBI, led by agent Tom White. The investigation exposes deep-seated greed, racism, and corruption in early 20th-century America. The film explores themes of betrayal, exploitation, and justice amidst a tragic chapter in American history.

3. past lives

past lives- movie summary

Past Lives (2023), directed by Celine Song, is a poignant romantic drama that explores the concepts of fate, missed connections, and the passage of time. The story follows two childhood friends from South Korea, Nora and Hae Sung, who are separated when Nora’s family immigrates to the United States. Over the next two decades, they reconnect twice—once in their twenties and again in their thirties—while living vastly different lives.
The film delves into the emotional complexity of their relationship, examining what could have been and the lives they’ve built apart. As Nora, now married to an American man, and Hae Sung reflect on their deep bond and shared past, the film raises questions about love, destiny, and the paths we choose. Past Lives is a contemplative and emotionally rich exploration of relationships, memory, and the passage of time.

4. Asteroid City

Asteroid City- movie summary

Asteroid City (2023), directed by Wes Anderson, is a whimsical and visually stylized film set in a fictional 1950s desert town where a junior stargazers' convention is being held. The story follows a group of eccentric characters—scientists, students, parents, and military personnel—who gather for this event, only to experience an unexpected alien encounter that leads to the town being quarantined. The film blends Anderson's signature quirky humor, meticulous visual design, and heartfelt moments as it explores themes of isolation, human connection, and the search for meaning in a world of uncertainty. With an ensemble cast and offbeat narrative, Asteroid City is a charming, surreal meditation on life, love, and the universe.

5. Poor Things

Poor Things- movie summary

Poor Things (2023), directed by Yorgos Lanthimos, is a darkly comedic and surreal reimagining of the Frankenstein story, based on the novel by Alasdair Gray. The film follows Bella Baxter, a young woman brought back to life by a brilliant but eccentric scientist, Dr. Godwin Baxter, after her tragic death. With a new life and an uninhibited sense of curiosity, Bella embarks on a journey of self-discovery, love, and freedom, defying societal norms. As Bella explores the world, she grapples with questions of identity, desire, and independence. Her character evolves from an innocent, childlike state to a bold, empowered woman determined to shape her own destiny. Poor Things is a visually striking, absurd, and thought-provoking exploration of personal transformation and liberation.
